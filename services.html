Tools and services I genuinely recommend

This page is the shortlist I actually use to run projects, stay sharp, and keep experimentation fun. When a link is a referral, I’ll call out the perk so it’s clear what you get and, when relevant, what I receive in return. Nothing here is pay-to-play.

I only include products I either pay for personally or rely on weekly. If you spot something that no longer belongs, please let me know so I can keep it honest.

  • Railway

    I ship prototypes and personal tools on Railway because the dashboard is simple, provisioning is fast, and the defaults stay out of the way until I need more control. It’s perfect when I want to test an idea without wiring up infrastructure from scratch.

    Perk: Railway adds usage credits to your account (and mine) when you join through this link, which helps offset your first deployments.

  • Wealthsimple

    Wealthsimple is the investing app I recommend to friends who want a clean interface, automatic portfolio rebalancing, and solid long-term ETF options without getting lost in jargon. Their app makes it painless to track contributions across registered and non-registered accounts.

    Perk: Their referral program usually offers a cash bonus once you fund your account, and it sends a smaller credit my way too.

How I keep this list up to date

Software stacks change often. I review this page quarterly to make sure the picks still earn their spot, and I’ll remove anything that starts to coast on hype. If I discover a better alternative, I’ll update the copy and the link right away.
